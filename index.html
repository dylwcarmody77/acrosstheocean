Across the Ocean: My Mother's Journey

Across the Ocean: My Mother's Journey

Sitting in an old chair, out the back of my grandfather’s old house, is a small woman. She sits in jeans, a knit jumper and thongs, with a cup of coffee in one hand, the mug with a large design of the Australian flag imprinted into it.

Her accent is broad Australian drawl, her humour ranges from mum jokes to incredibly crude and she doesn’t mind a Corona or two.

She is Australian as they come, but in reality, she’s not.

48 years before, on a small rickety plane rattling over the Indian Ocean, she, her two brothers and her mother made the journey to Australia, all the way from the hot dust of Chennai, India.
