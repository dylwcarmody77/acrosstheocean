Across the Ocean: My Mother's Journey

48 Years On

Are you happy?

Nearly half a century since she moved her, Andrea Carmody has forged a pretty good life for herself. Teaching psychology and history at South Oakleigh Secondary College, she has travelled the world, had two children and been married for 22 years. It’s a testament to those chasing the dream, evidence that a move to Australia, or America, or England, can pay dividends. You can get a better education, find better job opportunities, and have a better life. “I’m lucky to have led such a good life so far,” Mum tells me, in a final word. Smiling in the dying sunlight of the day, she sits back on the couch, and past me, almost gazing back into the past. “I have a loving husband, two beautiful kids, and a family who loves me. I’m lucky I moved, I’m lucky my family moved.” She smiles at me. “Its my journey. And I wouldn’t have it any other way.”
